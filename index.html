Pascal's Law
Pressure applied to fluid transmits equally in all directions — the core science behind every HydraBot movement
Fluid Mechanics
How liquids transfer force through hoses and cylinders to create controlled motion
Hydraulic Systems
Understanding how hydraulic cylinders convert liquid pressure into mechanical force and movement
Forces & Motion
Applying push/pull forces through joysticks to control 6 joints and observe Newton's Laws in action
Friction & Lubrication
Applying hydraulic oil to O-rings and pistons reduces friction for smooth, efficient movement
Real-World Connections
Hydraulics power construction equipment, car brakes, airplane landing gear, and even dentist chairs
Scientific Method
Testing, observing, and troubleshooting when parts don't work as expected builds experimental thinking
Measurement
Cutting hoses to exact lengths (5.5cm, 13cm, 15cm, 15.5cm) using a ruler and marker for precise fits
Geometry & Angles
Operating joints at precise angles: 40°, 45°, 90°, 180°, and 270° base rotation
Counting & Sorting
Organizing and counting 220 parts across 8 labeled trays (A–H) before and during assembly
Spatial Reasoning
Reading 3D exploded diagrams and visualizing how flat parts connect to form a complex mechanical arm
Sequential Logic
Following numbered steps in exact order — skipping steps causes errors, reinforcing logical sequencing
Ratios & Proportions
Mechanical advantage shows how a small force in the joystick creates a larger force at the gripper
Engineering Design Process
Following a structured build process: plan → assemble → test → troubleshoot → improve
Reading Technical Diagrams
Interpreting exploded-view assembly diagrams, part labels, and callout arrows used in real engineering
Systems Thinking
Understanding how 6 independent hydraulic systems work together as one complete robotic arm
Mechanical Engineering
Joints, levers, pistons, cylinders, and gears — core components of real mechanical systems
Troubleshooting & Debugging
Diagnosing why a joint won't move — checking hoses, air bubbles, O-rings, and connections systematically
Precision & Quality Control
Tightening every screw fully, clean burr removal, and correct O-ring orientation — tolerances matter
Human-Machine Interaction
Using 5 joystick levers to control 6 axes of the robot arm — just like industrial robotic systems
